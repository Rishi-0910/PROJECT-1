Delhi

The Taj Mahal is one of the most iconic monuments in the world, located in Agra, India.

Varanasi

Varanasi, one of the oldest living cities in the world, is a spiritual hub in India,for its ghats along the Ganges River, where people come to perform religious rituals and seek salvation.

Pink city

Jaipur, often referred to as the "Pink City," is renowned for its distinctive pink-hued architecture, vibrant culture, and rich history as the capital of Rajasthan.

Kerala

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," is a picturesque state in southern India famous for its lush landscapes, tranquil backwaters, pristine beaches, and rich cultural heritage.

Ooty

Ooty, also known as Udhagamandalam, is a popular hill station in Tamil Nadu, renowned for its scenic landscapes, cool climate, lush green tea gardens, and charming colonial-era architecture, making it a favored destination for nature lovers and tourists.

jagannath mandir

The Jagannath Temple, located in Puri, Odisha, is a famous Hindu pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Jagannath, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, and is renowned for its grand annual Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival), attracting millions of devotees from around the world.




Delhi

Morning: Visit to Taj Mahal

Start your day with a morning visit to the Taj Mahal (8:00 am - 10:00 am). Arrive early to avoid the crowds and heat. Purchase your tickets online or at the ticket counter to skip the lines. Go through the security check and deposit any prohibited items. Spend a couple of hours exploring the stunning architecture, gardens, and reflecting pools.

Lunch Break

After visiting the Taj Mahal, head to one of the recommended lunch spots (11:00 am - 1:00 pm) for a delicious meal with a view. Hotel Saniya Palace or Shankara Vegis are great options. Savor your meal while taking in the breathtaking views of the Taj Mahal. Try local specialties, such as Mughlai or North Indian dishes, to experience the authentic flavors of Agra.

Afternoon: Explore Agra

In the afternoon (2:00 pm - 5:00 pm), explore the city of Agra. Visit the Agra Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and former residence of the Mughal emperors. Wander through the fort's impressive architecture, gardens, and museums. Stop by the Jama Masjid, one of the largest mosques in India, and explore the local markets, such as the Kinari Bazaar or the Sadar Bazaar. Try some local street food to satisfy your cravings.

Varanasi

Morning: Visit Varanasi

Start your day early with a visit to the Ghats of Varanasi (6:00 am - 8:00 am). Take a sunrise boat ride along the Ganges River to witness the spiritual beauty of the city. Watch the locals perform their morning rituals and prayers along the ghats. You can also take a stroll along the ghats and explore the narrow alleys and streets of Varanasi

Lunch Break

After your morning excursion, head to a rooftop restaurant with a stunning view of the Ganges River and the ghats (12:00 pm - 1:30 pm). Some popular options include the rooftop restaurant at Hotel Ganges Grand, Hotel Hindusthan International, or the Kesar Heritage Hotel. Savor local delicacies such as banarasi thandai, kachoris, and jalebis while taking in the breathtaking views of the city.

Afternoon: Explore Varanasi

In the afternoon (2:00 pm - 5:00 pm), explore the narrow alleys and streets of Varanasi. Visit the Kashi Vishwanath Temple, one of the most revered Shiva temples in India. Explore the local markets, such as the Vishwanath Gali and the Thatheri Bazaar, to experience the vibrant culture and traditions of Varanasi. You can also visit the Banaras Hindu University, one of the largest residential universities in Asia

Pink City

Morning: Visit Pink city

Start your day early by visiting the iconic Hawa Mahal (6:00 am - 8:00 am), also known as the Palace of Winds. This stunning pink sandstone monument is a masterpiece of Rajput architecture. Take a stroll around the nearby markets, such as the Johari Bazaar and the Bapu Bazaar, to experience the vibrant culture and traditions of Jaipur.

Lunch Break

After your morning excursion, head to a rooftop restaurant with a stunning view of the city (12:00 pm - 1:30 pm). Some popular options include the rooftop restaurant at the Jaipur Modern Kitchen. Savor local delicacies such as dal baati churma, laal maans, and ghevar while taking in the breathtaking views of the Pink City.

Afternoon: Explore Varanasi

In the afternoon (2:00 pm - 5:00 pm), explore the magnificent Amber Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Take an elephant ride up to the fort, which offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Visit the Jantar Mantar, an ancient astronomical observatory, and the City Palace, which showcases the rich history and culture of Jaipur. Don't forget to try some local street food, such as pyaaz kachoris and mirchi bada, to satisfy your cravings.






kerala

Morning: Visit Kerala

Start your day early with a tranquil boat ride on the backwaters of Alleppey (6:00 am - 8:00 am). Watch the sunrise over the lush green landscapes and witness the daily life of the locals. You can also visit the Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, home to a wide variety of migratory birds.

Lunch Break

After your morning excursion, head to a local restaurant with a stunning view of the backwaters (12:00 pm - 1:30 pm). Some popular options include the restaurants at the Kumarakom Lake Resort, the Alleppey Beach Resort, or the Houseboat restaurants. Savor local delicacies such as karimeen, appam, and stew while taking in the breathtaking views of the backwaters.

Afternoon: Explore kerala

In the afternoon (2:00 pm - 5:00 pm), explore the scenic hill station of Munnar. Visit the Tea Museum to learn about the history of tea plantations in Kerala. Take a stroll around the Mattupetty Dam and the Echo Point, which offer stunning views of the surrounding landscapes. Don't forget to try some local tea and snacks, such as chai and pazham pori, to satisfy your cravings.

Ooty

Morning: Visit Ooty

Start your day early with a scenic drive to the Doddabetta Peak (6:00 am - 8:00 am), the highest point in Ooty. Watch the sunrise over the lush green Nilgiri Hills and witness the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. You can also visit the Botanical Garden, home to a wide variety of flora and fauna

Lunch Break

After your morning excursion, head to a local restaurant with a stunning view of the valley (12:00 pm - 1:30 pm). Some popular options include the restaurants at the Fern Hill Palace, the Hotel Darshan, or the Earl's Secret. Savor local delicacies such as idlis, dosas, and vadas while taking in the breathtaking views of the valley

Afternoon: Explore kerala

In the afternoon (2:00 pm - 5:00 pm), explore the scenic Ooty Lake, a popular spot for boating and fishing. Take a stroll around the lake and enjoy the picturesque views of the surrounding hills. You can also visit the nearby Deer Park and the Government Museum to learn about the history and culture of Ooty. Don't forget to try some local tea and snacks, such as chai and homemade chocolates, to satisfy your cravings.

Jagannath mandir

Morning: Visit Jagannath mandir

Start your day early with a visit to the Jagannath Temple (6:00 am - 8:00 am), one of the most sacred Hindu temples in India. Watch the sunrise over the majestic temple structure and witness the daily rituals and prayers. Take a stroll around the temple complex and explore the various shrines and mandapas.

Lunch Break

After your morning darshan, head to a local restaurant near the temple (12:00 pm - 1:30 pm) to savor some delicious Odia cuisine. Try some local specialties like khichdi, dalma, and kanika while taking in the vibrant atmosphere of the temple town. Some popular restaurants include the Jagannath Temple Canteen, the Puri Hotel, or the Loknath Restaurant.

Afternoon: Explore Jagannath mandir

In the afternoon (2:00 pm - 5:00 pm), explore the nearby Bada Danda, a bustling marketplace filled with colorful shops and stalls selling everything from souvenirs to local handicrafts. Take a stroll along the beach and enjoy the sunset over the Bay of Bengal. You can also visit the nearby Gundicha Temple, a sacred spot where Lord Jagannath is said to rest during the annual Rath Yatra festival.
